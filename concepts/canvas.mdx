The canvas is the top-level workspace in NodePad. Every chat thread you start, every branch you fork, and every synthesis you merge lands here — arranged in two dimensions so you can see the shape of your thinking at a glance, not just the most recent reply.

## Why a canvas instead of a chat window

Most AI apps give you one thread: a vertical scroll of messages from top to bottom. That works fine for a single question, but it breaks down when your work spreads across multiple topics, approaches, or models. You end up juggling browser tabs, losing context between sessions, or — worse — letting a promising tangent die because there was no clean place to put it.

The canvas solves this by adding a second dimension. Instead of a single line of messages, you get a surface where threads can be neighbors. A research session might have three threads running in parallel. An engineering decision might have a main thread with two forks for second opinions. You see all of it at once, spatially arranged the way the problem actually fits in your head.

<Tip>
  Think of the canvas the way you'd think of a physical whiteboard: individual threads are like clusters of sticky notes, and you can step back to see how everything relates before zooming in to work on a specific corner.
</Tip>

## What lives on the canvas

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Chat threads" icon="messages">
    Each thread is a sequence of nodes — messages and replies — organized vertically. Threads are the primary unit of work on the canvas.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Forked branches" icon="code-branch">
    When you fork a node, a new thread grows from that point. The branch appears on the canvas beside its parent, sharing the same context up to the fork.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Synthesis nodes" icon="merge">
    When you merge two threads, a synthesis node captures the reconciled result. It lives on the canvas alongside the threads that produced it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sticky notes" icon="note-sticky">
    Sticky notes attach directly to nodes. They're visible on the canvas as a persistent reminder of the constraints in effect for that thread.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Navigating the canvas

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pan to explore">
    Click and drag any empty area of the canvas to move around. Threads don't snap to a grid — place them wherever the layout makes sense to you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Zoom to focus">
    Scroll or pinch to zoom in on a specific thread or zoom out to see your full workspace at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open any thread">
    Click a node in any thread to bring it into focus and continue the conversation from that point.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Canvases and sessions

Each canvas represents a working session or project. You can create multiple canvases — one per project, client, or research question — and switch between them without losing the spatial layout you've built up.
